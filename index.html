Many farms in Denmark transport cattle to other countries within the EU and Middle East, these journey’s can last for days and cause great distress on the animal. Cattle transportation is rising concern as the welfare of the cattle is most of the time overlooked.

Enclosed

Meet Carl, he’s a 10 month old young cattle. Welcome to his home, here you see him in his only living space he has ever known. Just the same dull grey flooring with the dark walls. Carl has his buddies, many of them, so many that it can become hard to move around or get any personal space.

Did you know?

The calves are sent for slaughter when they are under 10 months old. Cattle are slaughtered at an age between 10 - 24 months.

Pushed

Just 10 months old, Carl’s final journey begins. Carl and his friends are pushed up against each other towards a narrow corridor to reach their spot on the cattle truck. The sense of fresh air and the bright light startles Carl, who has lived his entire life in closed doors. The sense of panic in the air makes Carl feel anxious and fear the unknown yet to come.

Trapped

Suddenly he finds himself in the middle of the truck surrounded by others trying to figure out their fate. More cattle tumble onto the vehicle, shoving him even further back, crammed together with others. He cries fo help but nothing changes. The fear spreads through the crowd and his friends are anxious and start getting aggressive.

Thirst & Hunger

It has felt like a lifetime being trapped on the truck and his tongue is beginning to become dry, while also longing for something to eat. He looks around but nothing is to be seen but all of the terrified faces of his friends. He begins to feel weak from dehydration and leans against the cold metal bar at the side of this unfamiliar enclosure.

Overheating

As the blazing sun is baking down on the roof of the truck, the heat quickly comes unbearable. The temperature causes Carl to overheat and he feels his legs losing the strength to hold him. He tries to stay standing but the weakness overcomes him. He attempts to get back up but other bodies have filled the space and Carl has no energy to fight back.

Injured

As Carl lays on the floor, he being stepped on and kicked by the other cows countless times. Wounds are opening up all over his body. Flies swarm to his injuries and blood seeps out onto the floor of the truck. Nobody is there to help him.

Disabled

After being on the road for over 24 hours, Carl is unable to stand up due to his infected wounds. After being forced by the butchers hand he manages to limp out of the truck into yet another closed space. This time he’s not leaving his new enclosure and is only waiting to meet his end and reach the shelves of the supermarket.
